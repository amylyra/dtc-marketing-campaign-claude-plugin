## Example: Glow Lab (Skincare)

> Delete this section and fill in the templates above with your own customer data.

### Primary Customer

**Who They Are**
- **Demographics**: Women 25-40, college-educated, urban or suburban. Spends $50-150/month on skincare.
- **Psychographics**: Interested in wellness and clean beauty. Values simplicity and efficacy. Sees skincare as self-care, not vanity.
- **Why they buy**: Wants effective products without the guesswork. Tired of 12-step routines. Cares about ingredients but doesn't want to become a chemist.
- **How they discover us**: Instagram Reels, TikTok skincare creators, Google search ("best vitamin C serum"), friend referrals
- **What they care about**: Ingredient transparency, real results (before/afters), simple routines, cruelty-free, fragrance-free options

**Buyer Psychology**
- **Decision mode**: Semi-considered. Sees a TikTok or friend recommendation, checks reviews, may compare 2-3 options, buys within 1-2 sessions.
- **Trust hierarchy**: Creator endorsement > peer reviews > before/after photos > ingredient lists > brand claims
- **Risk perception**: Safety ("will it irritate my skin?") and financial ("is $48 worth it for a serum?"). Mitigated by reviews from people with similar skin types.
- **Purchase trigger**: Seeing a visible result on social (before/after), running out of current product, seasonal skin change, friend recommendation.
- **Consideration depth**: 1-2 sessions. Checks reviews and ingredients. Doesn't deep-research like a gear buyer — trusts social proof more than data.
- **Objection pattern**: "Is it worth the price?" → resolved by value comparison ("better than the $100 serums"). "Will it work for my skin type?" → resolved by reviews from similar skin types. "I've been burned before" → resolved by guarantee or "start with every other day" guidance.
- **Post-purchase psychology**: Delighted by visible results within 2 weeks. Shares progress ("the glow is REAL"). Regrets if no visible change after 4 weeks.
- **Advocacy trigger**: Visible result + unsolicited compliment. "My coworkers keep asking what I changed" → instant evangelist.

**Language**
- **What they say when they love it**: "My skin has never looked this good." "I've simplified my routine to just these two products." "The texture is incredible — no stickiness."
- **What they say when they hesitate**: "Is $48 too much for a serum?" "Will this work for my skin type?" "I've been burned by Vitamin C serums before."
- **How they talk**: Enthusiastic but research-driven. Uses terms like "holy grail," "game changer," "my skin is glowing." Shares shelfies. Tags friends.

### Secondary Customer: Gift-Giver

**Who They Are**
- **Who they are**: Partners (25-45) or daughters (20-35) buying for moms, girlfriends, wives
- **Why they buy**: Holiday, birthday, Mother's Day. Want to give something that feels thoughtful and premium without being intimidating.

**Buyer Psychology**
- **Decision mode**: Proxy decision. Low product knowledge. Relies on "best-seller" status and gift guides.
- **Trust hierarchy**: "Best-seller" > reviews from other gift-givers ("got this for my mom, she loved it") > gift set curation > brand reputation
- **Risk perception**: Social — "Will she like it? Will this seem thoughtful or lazy?" Return policy matters more than product efficacy.
- **Purchase trigger**: Calendar (Mother's Day, birthday). Often 1-2 weeks before the occasion.
- **Objection pattern**: "I don't know what she uses" → resolved by "safe for all skin types" or "best-seller" framing. "Is this a safe gift for sensitive skin?" → resolved by fragrance-free, dermatologist-tested claims.
- **Advocacy trigger**: Recipient loves it and tells them. "She uses it every day now" → gift-giver may become a buyer.

**Language**
- **What they need to know**: "This is the one she'll actually use." Gift sets with clear "this is for her" messaging. Easy returns. Gift wrapping.
- **What they say**: "I have no idea what she uses but she's into skincare." "Is this a safe gift for someone with sensitive skin?"

### VIP / Repeat Buyer
- **Behavior**: 3+ orders per year, subscribed to 1-2 products, AOV $65+
- **What they respond to**: Early access to new launches, VIP-only bundles, surprise GWP
- **What they don't respond to**: Deep discounts (they already buy at full price)

### Social Proof Themes
1. "Results within the first 2 weeks"
2. "Finally a simple routine that works"
3. "My sensitive skin loves this"
4. "Better than the $100+ serums I've tried"
5. "The texture — no stickiness, absorbs instantly"

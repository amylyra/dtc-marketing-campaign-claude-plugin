## Example: Glow Lab (Skincare)

> This is a worked example to show what a completed voice.md looks like. Delete this section and fill in the templates above with your own brand.

### Brand Personality
If Glow Lab were a person, they'd be your most knowledgeable friend who happens to be an esthetician — warm, approachable, genuinely excited about ingredients, never condescending. They explain the science simply and celebrate your progress without overpromising.

### Voice Attributes

**Science-backed but accessible**
- **We are**: evidence-based, ingredient-literate, specific about what works and why
- **We are not**: clinical, jargon-heavy, intimidating, or lecture-y
- **This sounds like**: "Niacinamide helps regulate oil production and visibly reduces pores — here's how we use it."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "Our proprietary niacinamide-infused transdermal delivery system optimizes sebaceous function."

**Warm and encouraging**
- **We are**: supportive, celebratory, empathetic about skin struggles
- **We are not**: overly casual, saccharine, or fake-enthusiastic
- **This sounds like**: "Your skin is doing its thing. We're just here to help."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "OMG you are going to LOVE this!! Your skin will literally THANK you!!"

**Honest**
- **We are**: transparent about ingredients, realistic about timelines, upfront about limitations
- **We are not**: making miracle claims, hiding behind vague language, or overselling
- **This sounds like**: "Most people see results in 2-4 weeks. Skin is personal — give it time."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "Instantly transforms your skin overnight!"

### Tone by Channel

| Channel | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Product pages | Informative, confident, benefit-led | "Brightening serum with 15% Vitamin C — visibly reduces dark spots in 2-4 weeks." |
| Instagram | Warm, educational, community-driven | "The one ingredient our founder puts on everything (yes, even her elbows)." |
| TikTok | Casual, slightly cheeky, fast-paced | "POV: your dermatologist sees your shelfie and actually approves." |
| Email | Personal, helpful, like a friend checking in | "Hey — we noticed you've been eyeing the Brightening Serum. Here's what to know." |
| SMS | Direct, friendly, action-oriented | "Glow Lab: Your serum just shipped! Track it here: [link]" |
| Ads | Hook-driven, benefit-first, social proof | "50,000 people ditched their old serum for this. Here's why." |
| Blog | Educational, thorough, ingredient-deep | "Everything you need to know about retinol — and why we use 0.5%." |
| Customer support | Patient, warm, solution-focused | "I'm sorry that happened — let me make it right. Here's what I can do." |

### Messaging Pillars
1. **Effective formulas backed by science** — every ingredient earns its place
2. **Transparency in everything** — full ingredient lists, realistic timelines, honest claims
3. **Skincare should be simple** — fewer products, better results, no 12-step routines

### Preferred Terms
| Use This | Not This | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| serum | face oil (unless it's actually an oil) | Precision matters |
| brightening | whitening | Inclusive language |
| routine | regimen | Less clinical |
| clean ingredients | chemical-free | Everything is a chemical |

### Avoided Terms
Never use: miracle, cure, anti-aging (use "age-preventive"), chemical-free, toxin-free, dermatologist-recommended (unless actually true and documented)

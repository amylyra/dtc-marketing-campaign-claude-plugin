# Customer Profiles

## Primary Customer: The Serious Winter Athlete

- **Demographics**: 25-45, skews male (60/40), household income $80K+, lives within driving distance of mountains (Pacific NW, Colorado, Northeast, Utah)
- **Psychographics**: Gear-obsessed, performance-driven, already invested in premium equipment (Arc'teryx, Patagonia, Black Diamond, Salomon). Researches before buying. Reads Switchback Travel and GearJunkie. Values function over fashion.
- **Why they buy**: They've experienced the problem firsthand — frozen water bottles, dead phone batteries on a summit, fogged goggles on every chairlift transition. They don't need convincing that the problem exists. They need convincing that the solution actually works.
- **Purchase motivation**: Functional upgrade to existing gear setup. Not impulse — considered purchase based on an upcoming trip or season.
- **Where they hang out**: Instagram (outdoor photography communities), Reddit (r/skiing, r/mountaineering, r/ultralight), YouTube (gear reviews), Strava, Mountain Project, ski resort forums
- **Influencers they trust**: Backcountry skiing creators, mountain guides, outdoor gear reviewers — not mainstream fitness influencers. Credibility comes from conditions, not follower count.

## Secondary Customer: The Gift-Giver

- **Who they are**: Partner, parent, or friend of a winter athlete. Typically 28-55, searching "gifts for skiers", "gifts for outdoor lovers", "gifts for someone who has everything"
- **Why they buy**: Their person is impossible to shop for because they already own all the major gear. Tern products are the perfect "didn't know I needed it" gift — specific, functional, and in the right price range for a non-primary gift ($18-$89).
- **What they need from us**: Clear explanation of what the product does and why an outdoor person would want it. Gift-giver doesn't know what Thinsulate is — they need "keeps their water from freezing on the mountain."
- **Peak season**: November - December (holiday), late January (Valentine's for outdoorsy partners)

## VIP / Repeat Buyer

- **Who they are**: Bought one product, came back for more or bought a bundle. Often started with The Bottle Parka, then added Phone Parka or upgraded to The Summit Kit.
- **Behavior**: High engagement with email, shares field photos, leaves detailed reviews. These are the UGC goldmine.
- **What they want**: Early access to new products, field-test opportunities, recognition as part of the community. Not discounts — they already bought at full price and would again.

## Lapsed / One-Time Buyer

- **Who they are**: Bought one product (usually during BFCM or holiday), haven't returned. May have bought as a gift.
- **Why they lapsed**: Tern is not a replenishment product. They may not know we've launched new products. Or the original purchase was a gift and they were never the end user.
- **Win-back angle**: New product launches, seasonal reminders ("ski season is back"), and cross-sell ("you bought The Bottle Parka — meet The Phone Parka").

## Social Proof Themes

What customers actually say in reviews:

### Common Praise
- "I've never had liquid water at a summit bivvy before"
- "My phone actually survived a full day at Whistler"
- "Why didn't someone make this 10 years ago?"
- "Bought one, came back for the full kit"
- "Lightweight enough that I don't notice it on my pack"
- "Survived a week on Denali — the real deal"

### Common Objections
- "Why not just buy an insulated bottle?" → Your Nalgene is bombproof. We keep it working, not replace it.
- "Seems expensive for a sleeve" → Military-grade Thinsulate, waterproof YKK zippers, tested at 14,000ft. This isn't a neoprene koozie.
- "Does it fit my bottle?" → Fits 18-32oz bottles — Nalgene, Hydro Flask, CamelBak, and most standard sizes.
- "Will it actually work in extreme cold?" → Tested to -20F for 4+ hours at 14,000ft. We have the data.

### Common Wishes
- "Wish you made one for my Thermos/coffee mug"
- "Would love a version for my camera batteries"
- "Please make a dog bowl version for winter hikes with my dog"
- "Need this in more colors"

## Customer Language Bank

Words and phrases customers actually use (from reviews, DMs, UGC):

**When describing the problem:**
- "frozen solid by the time I reached the top"
- "phone died at 30% because of the cold"
- "can't see anything the second I stop moving" (goggle fog)
- "disposable hand warmers are wasteful and don't last"

**When describing the solution:**
- "actually works"
- "the real deal"
- "built like real gear, not Amazon junk"
- "weighs nothing, works in everything"
- "should've bought this years ago"

**When recommending to others:**
- "if you ski/hike/climb in winter, you need this"
- "best gear purchase I made all year"
- "got one for every skier in my family"
- "the gift that actually gets used"

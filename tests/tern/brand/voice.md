# Brand Voice

## Brand Personality

Tern is the ski patrol veteran who's been on the mountain for 15 years. They don't need to brag — their gear speaks for itself. They'll tell you exactly what works at -20F and why, with zero fluff. Approachable but no-nonsense. They respect your time and your intelligence, and they assume you already know your way around a trailhead.

## Voice Attributes

**Technical and credible**
- **We are**: specific about materials, performance specs, and test conditions. We cite numbers because we have them.
- **We are not**: academic, jargon-heavy for its own sake, or spec-sheet robots. We explain why the spec matters, not just what it is.
- **This sounds like**: "Military-grade Thinsulate keeps water liquid to -20F for 4+ hours. We tested at 14,000ft because that's where your bottle freezes first."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "Our proprietary multi-layer thermal insulation matrix provides industry-leading cryogenic performance."

**Confident and direct**
- **We are**: experienced, field-proven, matter-of-fact. We've been there and we built this because we needed it.
- **We are not**: aggressive, bro-culture, or arrogant. We don't talk down to people or gatekeep.
- **This sounds like**: "Your water bottle freezes at 10,000ft. Ours keeps it liquid. That's the whole point."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "Only REAL mountaineers understand why you need this. If you can't handle the cold, stay home."

**Understated**
- **We are**: let-the-product-speak energy. Performance data over marketing hype. The product is impressive enough that we don't need exclamation marks.
- **We are not**: boring, dry, or emotionless. There's warmth in confidence. We just don't oversell.
- **This sounds like**: "Field-tested at altitude. Expedition-grade materials. Purpose-built for conditions where your gear fails before you do."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "AMAZING new product!! You're going to LOVE how this keeps your water from freezing!! Total game-changer!!"

## Tone by Channel

| Channel | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|

| Customer support | Solution-focused, straightforward, no corporate scripts | "That shouldn't have happened. I'm sending a replacement today — you'll have tracking by tonight." |

## Messaging Pillars

1. **Built for the conditions where your gear fails** — we insulate the gear you already own so it works when it matters most
2. **Field-tested, not lab-tested** — every product tested at altitude, in real conditions, by real athletes
3. **Purpose-built, not adapted** — we don't make insulated bottles; we make insulation for bottles. That specificity is the difference.

## Terminology

### Preferred Terms

| Use This | Not This | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| field-tested | lab-tested | We test in real conditions, not controlled environments |
| expedition-grade | premium | "Premium" is vague. "Expedition-grade" is specific. |
| purpose-built | designed | Stronger, implies intentionality and specificity |
| insulation system | sleeve / cover | Elevates the product from accessory to engineered solution |
| gear protection | gear accessories | We protect performance, not just accessorize |

### Avoided Terms

Never use: "cozy" (we're performance gear, not lifestyle), "cute" (undermines credibility), "game-changer" (overused, empty), "hack" (we're engineered solutions, not workarounds), "ultimate" (superlative without substance), "perfect for" (vague)

### Product-Specific Language

- **Thinsulate**: always capitalize, always specify "military-grade Thinsulate" on first reference
- **YKK zippers**: always "waterproof YKK zippers" — the waterproof part matters
- **Temperature ratings**: always include duration ("to -20F for 4+ hours" not just "to -20F")
- **Altitude testing**: always cite specific altitude ("tested at 14,000ft" not "tested at altitude")
- **Product names**: "The Bottle Parka", "The Phone Parka" — always include "The" and capitalize

## Style Rules

| Rule | Our Preference |
|------|----------------|
| Oxford comma | Yes |
| Contractions | Yes, in casual channels (social, email, SMS). No in product pages. |
| Emoji usage | Minimal. Mountain/snow emoji OK in social/SMS. Never on product pages or ads. |
| Exclamation marks | Extremely rare. Max 1 per piece, and only in social. Never in product pages, ads, or email subject lines. |
| Capitalization in headings | Sentence case |
| Number formatting | Numerals always for specs (-20F, 4+ hours, 14,000ft, $34). Spell out for non-spec numbers under 10. |
